## 1. Problem Statement
Millions of Indian citizens face difficulties in accessing government schemes, public services, education, healthcare, and employment opportunities due to fragmented information systems, language barriers, low digital literacy, and complex digital portals. This results in low awareness, underutilization of welfare schemes, and high dependency on intermediaries.

## 2. Objective
The objective of SAHAYAK AI is to provide an AI-powered, multilingual, and conversational platform that enables citizens to easily discover, understand, and access public services through simple text or voice-based interaction in their preferred Indian language.

## 3. Target Users
- Rural and semi-urban citizens
- Low digital literacy users
- Marginalized and underserved communities
- First-time users of digital government platforms

## 4. Functional Requirements
- Multilingual text-based interaction
- Voice-based interaction (optional / future-ready)
- Natural language query understanding
- Collection of minimal user details (age, income, state, category)
- Eligibility-based scheme and opportunity recommendation
- Personalized and context-aware responses
- Step-by-step actionable guidance for applications

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements
- High scalability to support large user volumes
- Low latency responses
- Secure handling of user data
- Cost-efficient operation
- High availability and fault tolerance

## 6. Constraints
- Prototype developed using AWS Free Tier where possible
- No direct integration with government databases in MVP
- Internet connectivity required (offline support planned for future)

## 7. Success Metrics
- Improved awareness of relevant schemes
- Reduction in dependency on intermediaries
- Faster access to public services
- Increased digital inclusion
